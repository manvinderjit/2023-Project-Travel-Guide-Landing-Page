Explore Toronto With Manvinderjit

The Home of Blue Jays!

The Toronto Blue Jays are a Canadian professional baseball team based in Toronto. The Blue Jays compete in Major League Baseball as a member club of the American League East division. The team has its home games primarily at Rogers Centre.

Rich Cultural Diversity!

Toronto is famous for being the most multicultural city in the world. With about half of Toronto’s 6,000,000 population being born outside of Canada, this melting pot of a city has over 250 ethnicities that speak more than 170 languages.

Toronto International Film Festival!

Toronto has almost become a second Hollywood in recent years, with the city being a massive hub for film and TV production and other entertainment mediums like music and theatre. Toronto is also famous for a number of celebrities.

Popular Tourist Attractions Toronto

CN Tower

The CN Tower in downtown Toronto is a must-see landmark for all tourists. A 553.3 m-high concrete communications and observation tower completed in 1976 used for radio communications.

Harbour Front Center

The tranquil Harbourfront area on the shores of Lake Ontario offers waterfront parks and trails, plus galleries, theatres and literary events at the Harbourfront Centre.

Ripley's Aquarium

Ripley's Aquarium of Canada, in Toronto, Ontario, Canada, is a 135,000 square-feet incredible family attraction with more than 1.5 million gallons of water with marine and freshwater habitats from around the world.

Finding Accomodations

Not sure where to stay in Toronto? Fret not, because there are so many different places to stay in Toronto, depending on what neighbourhood you want to use as a home base to what your travel style and reasons for visiting might be. From luxe hotels downtown to hip living-like-a-local vacation rentals in trendy corners of the city off the beaten path, from tucked-away hotels to escape the crowds to nooks close to the airport to sleep off the jetlag, Toronto boasts a variety of lodging options for every type of travel and every kind of traveller. Places to stay within the city range from world-class hotels to budget hostels, from group-sized vacation rentals to cozy rental apartments perfect for solo travellers to quickly make Toronto feel like a home away from home so your time here is the best that it can be.

Hotels

Hotels in Toronto have been some of the most prominent buildings in the city and the hotel industry is one of the city's most important. The Greater Toronto Area has 183 hotels with a total of almost 36,000 rooms.

Apartment Rentals

Apartments are available on a short-term basis (from one month onwards) in apartment buildings or are part of a hotel through sites like Airbnb, Kayak or Trivago. The costs are far cheaper than staying in a comparable hotel suite.

Hostels

Hostelling International (HI) Canada operates 51 hostels coast-to-coast across Canada, including four hostels in Ontario one of which is in downtown Toronto. You can be any age and take advantage of staying in a hostel.
